A streamlined edition for newcomers — simplified from the historical "Philosophers' Game" devised by medieval monks to teach Boethian number theory.

How to Play

The goal: capture every one of your opponent's pieces, or maneuver three of your pieces into a mathematical Harmony on their side of the board. Whichever comes first wins.

Pieces & Movement

 Circle — moves exactly 1 square, any direction.
 Triangle — moves exactly 2 squares, any direction, in a straight line.
 Square — moves exactly 3 squares, any direction, in a straight line.
 Pyramid — your champion piece; moves like a Circle, 1 square any direction.

Pieces move the exact distance shown — never less — and cannot jump over other pieces. White (gold, even numbers) always moves first.

The Four Stratagems of Capture

Assault — move directly onto an enemy piece of exactly equal value, the way a chess piece captures. Rare, since your numbers are even and the enemy's are odd!
Ambush — surround an enemy piece with two or more of your own pieces (touching it on any side, including diagonally) whose values add up to or multiply to exactly its value. Example: a 5 and a 7 beside an enemy 12 (5+7=12) captures it.
Siege — if every open orthogonal side of an enemy piece (up/down/left/right) is occupied by your pieces, it is captured — boxed in with nowhere to go.
Sally — a piece can strike at range along a row, column, or diagonal without moving. Count the distance between the two pieces, including both squares. If your value × that distance equals the enemy's value (or divides evenly into it), you capture from afar. Example: your 8 is two squares from an enemy 16 (8×2=16) — captured.

After you move each turn, any Ambush, Siege, or Sally captures available on the board will glow — tap a glowing enemy piece to claim it. You may take as many as are available before ending your turn.

How to Win

Elimination — capture every one of your opponent's pieces.

Harmony (Triumph) — get three of your own pieces evenly spaced in a straight line (row, column, or diagonal), entirely on your opponent's side of the board, with values forming an arithmetic sequence (like 2, 4, 6), a geometric sequence (like 4, 8, 16), or a harmonic sequence (like 3, 4, 6). This was the medieval scholars' most celebrated way to win.

A note on this edition

The historical game includes multi-layered pyramids, several additional victory conditions, and regional rule variants collected over eight centuries of play. This edition keeps the mathematics and the four stratagems intact while simplifying piece counts and win conditions so newcomers can learn it in one sitting.
